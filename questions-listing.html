Explain quantum computing in simple terms?

Quantum computing is a type of computing that uses quantum-mechanical phenomena, such as superposition and entanglement, to perform operations on data. In traditional computing, a bit can only have two possible states: 0 or 1. In quantum computing, a quantum bit (qubit) can exist in a superposition of both states simultaneously, allowing for much faster computation.

Imagine you have a bunch of coins that you need to flip to get a specific outcome, but you can only flip one coin at a time. In traditional computing, you would have to flip each coin individually until you get the desired outcome. In quantum computing, you can flip all the coins at once, and they will exist in a superposition of both heads and tails until you measure them, collapsing the superposition into a single outcome.
